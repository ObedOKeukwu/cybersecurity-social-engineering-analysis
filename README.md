# cybersecurity-social-engineering-analysis

## Overview

This project demonstrates the application of reconnaissance and social engineering principles in a controlled academic environment. The objective was to analyze a target audience, identify psychological vulnerabilities, and develop a realistic phishing scenario while maintaining ethical and legal compliance.

The project focuses on understanding how attackers leverage publicly available information and human behavior to conduct phishing attacks and how organizations can defend against such threats through awareness, training, and security controls.

---

## Objectives

- Perform non-intrusive reconnaissance using publicly available information.
- Develop a target profile based on demographics and behavioral characteristics.
- Apply social engineering principles to create a realistic phishing scenario.
- Analyze why a target may be susceptible to phishing attacks.
- Recommend mitigation strategies to reduce phishing risk.
- Demonstrate ethical cybersecurity practices.

---

## Target Audience

**Prospective International Students**

### Characteristics
- Seeking admission to colleges and universities
- Working under application and visa deadlines
- Trust institutional communications
- Frequently interact with online portals and email systems

### Potential Vulnerabilities
- Urgency caused by application deadlines
- Trust in official-looking communications
- Stress during admission and visa processes
- Limited familiarity with security verification procedures

---

## Reconnaissance Techniques

The following non-intrusive sources were analyzed:

- University admissions websites
- Public student discussion forums
- Social media communities
- Publicly available admission process information

The information gathered was used to understand communication patterns, motivations, and potential attack vectors.

---

## Social Engineering Framework

The phishing scenario was designed using common social engineering principles:

### Authority
Use of official university branding and professional language.

### Urgency
Creating pressure through potential admission delays or offer cancellation.

### Trust
Imitating familiar admission and document submission processes.

### Social Proof
Referencing common procedures and requirements experienced by applicants.

---

## Phishing Scenario

A simulated phishing email was created targeting prospective international students.

### Attack Goal

- Encourage victims to click a link
- Create a sense of urgency
- Exploit trust in educational institutions

### Safety Controls

This project was conducted ethically.

- No malicious payloads were used.
- No credential collection occurred.
- All links redirected to legitimate educational websites.
- The exercise was performed solely for educational purposes.

---

## Security Analysis

The project demonstrates how attackers can exploit:

- Human trust
- Time-sensitive decisions
- Familiar organizational branding
- Lack of verification procedures

### Potential Impact

- Credential theft
- Financial fraud
- Identity theft
- Admission process disruption
- Reputational damage

---

## Mitigation Strategies

### User Awareness

- Security awareness training
- Phishing simulation exercises
- Verification of sender identities

### Technical Controls

- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- SPF, DKIM, and DMARC email protections
- Email filtering and anti-phishing controls
- Secure applicant portals

### Organizational Controls

- Official communication policies
- Verified payment procedures
- SMS or secondary verification for critical actions
